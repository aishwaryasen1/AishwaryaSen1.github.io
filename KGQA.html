End-to-end Knowledge Graph for Question Answering

Team Size : 4

Project Guide : Dr. Mansi A. Radke

Project Duration : Sep 2020 - Dec 2020

Motivation

Question-Answering (QA) is a fundamental sub-domain in Natural Language Processing (NLP), which uses information retrieval based on linguistic or semantic instincts, developed using Artificial Intelligence techniques, to return concise answers to queries in natural language. The rising popularity of QA systems can be attributed to their increasing necessity in combating the abundance of information in the digital age. Thus, the motivation behind this study arises from the need for a comprehensive and accurate QA system.

Summary

In this study, an end-to-end pipeline for Question-Answering over the Multi-RC dataset using knowledge graphs is introduced. The key challenge posed by this dataset is that it necessitates textual entailment approaches that go beyond lexical-level matching, by requiring information from the entire paragraph to answer a single question. This entails the performance of reasoning over multiple edges of the knowledge graph to arrive at the required answer, also termed as 'multi-hop' Knowledge Graph Question Answering (KGQA). The proposed pipeline follows a comprehensive, step-by-step approach to arrive at accurate answer/answers.
